BASOTHO TRADITIONAL DANCES

Watch and listen to the beautiful Basotho traditional dances and music

  1. Mokhibo(women`s dance)

    2. A traditional dance performed by by women,characterized by rhythmic shoulder movements. It is performed mostly when kneeling.

  2. MOHOBELO(Men`s dance)

    3. A high energy dance from men,featuring rhythimic stompimg,leaping, and prancing movements.

  3. LITOLOBONYA

    4. Litolobonya is traditional dance ritual performed by girl and married women,it is private, often following child birth

  4. MOKOROTLO

    5. This traditional male dance is performed for a chief during imporant events such as political meetings or inspections.

  5. NDLAMO

    6. This dance is played by young girls,they swing their legs in rhythm to the music

  6. LIPHOTHA

    7. This traditional dance is for males

  7. SELIA-LIA

    8. This one is played by young girls and boys,girls and boys stand in parallel lines,girls hold stickes singing and dancing toward to boys to propose marriage.

  8. KHATI

    9. This game is played by young girls uisng a skipping rope ,they sing while skipping a rope.

  9. MOQOQOPELO

    10. This is a dance for girls,they dance during traditional ceremonies and celebrations.

  10. BOLEKE

    11. It is played by boys and girls it is hide and seek.

  11. LEKOLULO

    12. A flute traditionally played by herding boys.

  12. SETOLO-TOLO

    13. A mouth bow resembling an extended jaw harp,played by men.

  13. THOMO

    14. A stringed instrument, a form of musical bow ,traditionally played by women.

  14. LEKOPE

    15. A stick pipe made from a hollowed red.

  15. LESIBA

    16. A string instrument consistiing of a string and feather,played by blowing over it.

  16. MAMOKHORONG( OR SEKHANKULA)

    17. Another herdboys` bow instrument,sometimes made with a tin can resonator.

  17. LEKHITLANE AND LENAKA

    18. Cow or goat horns used by traditonal doctors for spiritual purposes.

  18. SEKUPU

    19. A drum,use to accompany other music and dancing.

  19. SEKEBEKU

    20. A wooden or metal jaw harp.

  20. TSHIPU

    21. It is played by young boys,usually in ceremonies and certain celebration places.

  21. LIKETO

    22. This game is played by girls,using stones.

  22. MAMOKHETHI

    23. This is played by girls in an open space,using a small ball.
